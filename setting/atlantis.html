Atlantis

We are not thriving. True, our people live, but our culture is dying. We are like a stone the ocean beats against. With each passing year a little more of us is worn away.

Atlantis: the Lost Empire (2001)

Location

Meta: Atlantis is located in the modern-day Azores, Portugal.
Naturally, Atlantis is also under the sea, rather than on the surface.

Atlanteans consider the ocean to be as a comfortable prison, as it simultaneously presents the greatest threat to the Atlantean people (in the form of Leviathans and the Evergrowth), but also acknowledge that the difficulty of traveling to Atlanteans for most humans has also protected them.

Atlantean Biology

The suffocating weight of the ocean provides unique challenges for the Atlanteans, to which they had adapted to over the course of millennia.

Most humans are rather similar to surface-dwelling humans, except that most have gills and are pressurized to survive for a small amount of time under the crushing weight of the ocean.
Most Atlantean city-dwellers prefer not to stay in the ocean, but rather in the bubbles that surround the Atlantean cities.

Since the advent of the Evergrowth, some Atlanteans have exhibited mutations, likely from long-term or developmental Evergrowth exposure.
These Atlanteans feature animalistic or plant-like characteristics, such as scales or shells, with rare major modifications, like a crab's arm or the eyestalks of a slug.
While minor mutations are welcome for their potential beneficial correlations to military might, major mutations often result in discrimination, particularly in the upper echelons of Atlantis society.

Society

... and on the reclusive peoples that have sworn a vigil and battle against the depths - and occasionally, against each other.

Modern Atlantis is a far cry from its ancient counterpart, as Athana, the Undying Prophetess, had once commented.
Ancient practices yet remain, stemming from a respect for the ancients, but new practices have become more popular, particularly amongst the common populace.

Atlantis, as a whole, has been sunken in the depths of the sea for approximately two millennia, developing a culture and society that is reminiscent of ancient days, while creating an entirely new and alien perspective on humanity.

Chitons and Ancient Greek Culture

Ancient Greek culture remains as a snapshot of Ancient Greece of the city of Atlantis, before it sunk.

Chiton (Ancient Greek dress for men and women) remain a common sight, due to the sheer abundance of sea grasses that had been discovered to be an excellent alternative for linen, an oft used material for Doric chitons (the variant with open shoulders). This resulted in the unique cloth: seacloth, used for Atlantean chitons. The quality of one's chiton is often an excellent method by which to judge a person's wealth, as seacloth by made the most talented and trained weavers are pleasant to the hand and to the eye; some even providing a modicum of armor! However, it is also the case that a particularly unfortunate individual could also harvest and make seacloth by hand, which might result in an unpleasant, stiff cloth.

There is also a particular emphasis on the study of Greek battle tactics, resulting in a particularly notable Treatise, rather than on entirely intellectual studies.
Many Hellenists in ancient times had often compared Atlantis as a sister closer to Sparta than to Athena, even before The Great Sinking, though with some further developments on the technologies necessary for war, rather than training only as the Spartans did.

Social Structure

The strict hierarchical social structure of Ancient Atlantis is also no more - having been destroyed by necessity in the War for Atlantis.
Rather than the Ancient Greek social structure, where male citizens were placed at the top, all of the men and women of Atlantean descent are citizens.
Due to the massive casualties of the War of Atlantis, slaves (known as duoli) do not exist, since all served in the war.

Revolutions after the war reinforced the then-new social structure of Atlantis against the landed aristocrats (aristoi), who had not served in the War.
After many centuries, this social structure became relaxed to the degree that it is today: where all Atlanteans have the rights of a citizen.

However, foreigners, known as metics, are heavily looked down upon and are regarded with blatant suspicion at the best of times.

Family

Atlanteans are highly family-oriented. In fact, some families even go into combat together, with great success.

The stressing of the importance of family, loyalty to the group, and loyalty to the city in which an Atlantean is born in results in a highly loyal, family-oriented citizen, willing to sacrifice even their lives for the city in the sake of their family.

Moving to a different city is grounds for becoming the black sheep of the family at best, with disownment being fairly common - with some murders even being reported for this cause.
Families can range from merely being nuclear, as is more common in richer households and by tradition, or with the entirety of the lineage living together in barrack-like arrangements, which is an Atlantean trait derived from the extreme accentuation of the military.

Soldiers & Respect

Career soldiers are highly respected, and service in the military for a minimum of ten years is mandatory for all citizens of Atlantis, regardless of city (though some cities impose up to twenty years of mandatory service).

Persons of authority are less respected than the soldiers that they command, unless they are a high-ranking individual in the military.
Notably, politicians that do not have a concrete understanding of the military have little chance of succeeding in the harsh political games of Atlantis.

Books

To Face the Leviathan by Herodotur Atallus: A then-contemporary book regarding the War for Atlantis, with interviews from Triton, the Hunter of Legends and Athana, the Undying Prophetess.

Treatises
The Treatise on Atlantean Warfare Against External Threats by Athana, the Undying Prophetess: A living document, maintained by Athana, the Undying Prophetess, and updated with contributions from several Atlantean cities. Commonly referred to as "The Treatise," and extensively used when in active warfare against the Evergrowth (and the Cult) or the Leviathans. To choose not to contribute is frowned upon, and will often result in Athana expending less energy on protecting and maintaining the uncooperative city. Also, is the basis for several peace treaties after civil wars.
