What are the Leviathans

... and are they truly enemies?

The Leviathans are the ancient creatures of the deep sea that have lived, fed, and slumbered since time immemorial.

Origins & Discovery

There is no known origin for how the Leviathans came into existence, except perhaps by sheer strength and growth over time.
Regardless, what is well known is their terrifying strength and fearsome might. Any mature Leviathan could easily annihilate portions of the original sunken city of Atlantis - and a Leviathan had indeed shattered Atlantis in the War for Atlantis. Most Leviathans appear to be slightly hostile against humanoids, though this is likely due to territorial encroachment on the humanoids' part, rather than an instinctual reaction.

The first Sunken Atlanteans had first despaired when they had discovered that there was a Leviathan near where Ancient Atlantis had sunk, presuming their lives entirely forfeit.
However, Poseidon had intervened, likely in order to prolong the punishment that the Atlanteans had needed to undergo by empowering a certain prophetess and hero to fight in his name and glory for the survival of the Atlanteans.

Life Cycle

The life cycle of a Leviathan has not been completely analyzed by any one researcher, but Atlantis has maintained records of particular specimen of interest.

The typical life cycle of a Leviathan is as follows:

  • Born (by 0 years)
  • Infant (by 5 years)
  • Juvenile (by 2 decades)
  • Adolescent (by 1 century)
  • Young Adult (by 5 centuries)
  • Mature, or Adult (by 1 millennia)
  • Elder (presumed by 5 millennia)
  • Ancient (unknown, 30 known specimen)

There are no records of any Leviathan dying of old age.

Similarities to Mundane Creatures & "Roots"

Meta: The "roots" referred to in the following section are different from the "roots" discovered by the Roman Grand Library.
One refers to the similarly shaped species that a Leviathan may correspond to, while the other refers to a property inherent to innate spell-casters.

Most Leviathans appear to be nearly identical to otherwise mundane creatures, just that they are scaled up in mass. Curiously, this is not the case, as the internals of a Leviathan are vastly different - even down to the cellular level. However, the common term for determining the Leviathan's sub-race is to define them by their "root" species; the species that most closely resembles the Leviathan if scaled down.

The reason as to why Leviathans appear, visually, to be similar to mundane creatures is unknown.

Behavior

... thousands of researchers lost.

The recordings of researchers from other the millennia, detailed how Leviathans react to external stimuli, specifically regarding living beings.

Age

Leviathans are most aggressive when young, and often slumber entirely in the Elder and Ancient stages.

Other Leviathans

Highly territorial against different "root" species, but friendly with similar species.
Some Leviathans even display basic social structures, or even being gregarious.

Leviathans claim a territory, often near their parents' territories, by patrolling a large portion of the sea floor, whilst marking the surrounding area.
Curiously, this often attracts many species of fish that are otherwise unable to protect themselves simply for preferring occasional tributes to a single Leviathan, rather than being hunted by many predators.

Humanoids

Leviathans typically consider lone humanoids to be identical to a minor school of fish; usually ignored unless particularly hungry.
Squads of humanoids often raise the ire of young Leviathans, which typically treat them with the mild caution of a predator against a particularly defensible fish.
Armies are immediate reasons for most Leviathans to attack, resulting in Atlantean wars being highly well-routed or covert.

Evergrowth

The Evergrowth are a new threat to the Leviathans, of which most consider them to be identical to plants.
However, particularly powerful Evergrowth specimen are treated as equals, and are dealt with terrifying ferocity.

Rarely, some Leviathans even assimilate - or perhaps, are assimilated by - the Evergrowth.

The Leviathan Cult

... in the end, human greed leads to death.

Unlike the Evergrowth Cult, there have been several attempts at the formation of a Leviathan Cult. None of these attempts had ever resulted in any major conflict.

Unlike the Evergrowth, the Leviathans lack the ability to become symbiotic partners - they are incapable of it, nor do they provide strength or power in any manner.
Successful attempts have been made to control Leviathans, but even juveniles were able to eventually remove mental shackles; instincts also prevent them from becoming domesticated.
It is also the case that Leviathans are high-profile creatures, and are nearly impossible to hide for more than a few months - whether due to the size of the specimen, or by the sheer amount of food necessary to maintain them.

The few attempts at contacting older Leviathans have all resulted in death, and their inherently terrifying size and visage make them difficult to worship.
